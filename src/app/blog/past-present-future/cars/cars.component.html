A history of how cars have transformed and how they will continue to transform.

Taylor Engel. Jul 25th, 2017.

The car is a marvelous 120 plus year old invention but has only recently in the last decade gone through a rapid and radical transformation. How did we get here and where will we go?

The automobile is a marvelous 120 plus year old invention but the automobile and the industries it supplements is at the cusp of two simultaneous revolutions – electrification and autonomization of the automobile. It may seem crazy to make a claim that electric and autonomous vehicles will spur on a total revolution inside the automotive and transport industries - but it will do just that. To make more sense and justify that claim we need to take a look at where and how these revolutions started as well as what these revolutions will look like in the future.

Where it started

To get a good idea of where this all started we have to go all the way back to Germany in 1885 where many consider Karl Benz’s invention, the Motorwagen, as the origination of the automobile. While it’s a stretch to make many similarities of this vehicle to the current state of the automobile it certainly has some major, fundamental similarities. The Motorwagen has an internal combustion engine, wheels, brakes and a method of steering which you’d find in almost every car between then and now.

In the early days of the automobile there were many innovations. The early 1900s was the first experimentation with electric vehicles and it was relatively successful at some points accounting for a third of all automobiles. However, by 1920 electric vehicles were all but nonexistent due to what I’ll refer to as a petroleum age which was a revolution in its own right that changed the automotive and transport industries. Affordable and plentiful gasoline, mass production and additional advancements to internal combustion engines that made the economical and logical choice for nearly all consumers. The status quo had not been challenged until just recently.
